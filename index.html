Luis Caballero Mejías

Venezuelan engineer and professor

A Venezuelan engineer and professor, dedicated to research and teaching in Venezuela in the mid-20th century, leaving important legacies in the field of research.

Chronology of the life of Luis Caballero Mejias:

  • 1903 - Born in Caracas, Venezuela

  • 1925 - He moved to Santiago de Chile to study and graduate as a Mechanical Technician at the School of Arts and Crafts in that city, standing out for his interest, proactivity and excellent grades.

  • 1930 - He was appointed Head of Workshops at the National Shipyard located in Puerto Cabello, Venezuela, until approximately 1935.

  • 1935 - En ese mismo año es nombrado Director de la Escuela de Artes y Oficios para hombres de Caracas.

  • 1937 - He changed his institution by calling it the “Industrial Technical School”, which he led for more than 20 years.

  • 1942 - The United States Patent Office awards him patent no. 2,283,876 for the invention known as the “Nut Cracking Machine.”

  • 1954 - The Ministry of Development granted the only known patent to the invention called “Corn Dough Flour” or “Dehydrated Corn Dough” with number 5,176. as it appears in the Bulletin of Industrial and Commercial Property of Venezuela.

  • 1956 - Students protest in the streets against Marcos Pérez Jiménez, and Luis Caballero Mejías is accused of providing them protection, which causes his dismissal and the temporary closure of the Technical School.

  • 1959 - He died of a painful unknown illness in Caracas.
